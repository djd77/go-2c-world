Brasília

Uniquely modernist architecture and design

Brasília is the capital city of Brazil, and is located in the Centre-West region. It has a population of over 3 million people, making it the third-largest city in the country.

The city was designed by Lúcio Costa, and founded in 1960. Viewing the city from above reveals that the main layout of the city resembles a butterfly or aeroplane. The city's architecture has a unique mordernist and futuristic design, due to the architect Oscar Niemeyer who designed the majority of the buildings within the city.

Brasília houses the National Congress, the Supreme Federal Court, the presidential office and residence, various embassies, and numerous other governmental buildings.
